Plot

Abbott Elementary follows the lives of several teachers at Willard R. Abbott Elementary School, an underfunded public school in Philadelphia. Filmed as a documentary, the show records the countless challenges (and silly antics) that take place throughout the school year. Janine Teagues is a second-grade teacher, whose positivitely can get her into trouble. She greatly admires seasoned kindergarden teacher, Barbara Howard, and wants to badly to be respected by her. Of course, there is a "Will they; won't they?" storyline between Janine and Gregory Eddie, a substitute who eventually was hired full-time.

This show offers a balance between commentary about the state of the education system in America and well-written humor. The commentary is subtle but thought-provoking, revealing some tough truths about education that non-teachers are likely not aware of.
